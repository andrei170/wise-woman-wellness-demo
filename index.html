What is missing under the hood

The schema and authority layer the site never received.

The site reads well to a human visitor. To an AI crawler it reads as a generic clinic page with no machine-readable proof of who the clinician is or what she is credentialed to do. These are the gaps a reader can verify themselves by viewing the page source.

  • No Practitioner schema

    There is no structured data identifying Randi Mann, WHNP-BC, MSCP, APNP as the practitioner, her credentials, her years of practice, or her certifications. AI engines cannot infer authority they cannot read.

  • No MedicalBusiness or LocalBusiness schema

    The address, geographic service area, opening hours, and service catalog are not exposed in JSON-LD. The site is invisible to the structured-data pipeline that feeds local AI answers.

  • No Service schema per offering

    Bioidentical HRT, pellet therapy, perimenopause and menopause care, thyroid evaluation, weight management, and functional medicine are listed as plain text. None of them are tagged so an AI engine can answer the service-specific query directly.

  • No FAQ schema on the most-asked questions

    Every menopause-specialist practice gets the same first-visit questions. None of those answers are wrapped in FAQ structured data, which means the AI summarizer cannot lift them into its response.

  • No county or metro qualifiers in indexable copy

    "Brown County," "De Pere," and "Northeast Wisconsin" appear inconsistently or not at all in headings, meta descriptions, and alt text. Local-intent queries lose the location match.

  • No credential-anchored cornerstone page

    The NCMP credential is mentioned but never used as the spine of a dedicated authority page. There is no single URL an AI engine can cite when asked who the menopause-certified practitioner is in the region.

  • No internal linking pattern around credential or founding year

    Every relevant page should link back to the credential page and the founder bio with descriptive anchor text. Right now the linking pattern is flat and the credential never accrues internal authority.

  • No off-site authority refresh

    The Menopause Society directory listing, the Wisconsin Board of Nursing license record, and the Discovery Health listing are not aligned with the website's current language. AI engines pull conflicting summaries from these sources.

90-Day plan

How the visibility gap closes in four phases.

Each phase produces a deliverable that is visible to the practice owner the day it ships. Nothing in this plan requires a website rebuild and nothing in it requires Randi Mann, WHNP-BC, MSCP, APNP to write copy herself.

Phase 01
Days 1 to 14

Credential and schema foundation

  • Ship a dedicated NCMP credential page anchoring the WHNP-BC, MSCP, and APNP designations to one URL.
  • Install MedicalBusiness, Practitioner, and LocalBusiness JSON-LD across the site.
  • Tag each service page with Service schema and link each back to the credential page.
  • Reconcile the Menopause Society directory listing, Wisconsin Board of Nursing record, and Discovery Health listing.
Phase 02
Days 15 to 35

Local-intent capture

  • Add Brown County, De Pere, Green Bay metro, and Northeast Wisconsin qualifiers into headings, meta descriptions, and alt text.
  • Build a Brown County menopause specialist cornerstone page that consolidates location signals.
  • Publish FAQ schema covering the questions women already call the office to ask before booking.
  • Refresh the Google Business Profile description and service categories to match the new on-site language.
Phase 03
Days 36 to 60

Service-specific authority pages

  • Build dedicated long-form pages for bioidentical HRT, pellet therapy, perimenopause care, and thyroid evaluation.
  • Anchor each page to the NCMP credential and link it back to the founder bio.
  • Add a published-and-reviewed-by byline using the full credential string on every clinical page.
  • Generate citation-friendly question-and-answer modules on each page for AI engines to lift.
Phase 04
Days 61 to 90

Reinforcement and re-audit

  • Re-run the five-query audit across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview and track the deltas.
  • Add an Article schema layer on the founder bio so authorship signals propagate.
  • Refresh the Instagram bio link, link-in-bio page, and any third-party directory listings to point to the new cornerstone URLs.
  • Hand the practice a one-page citation report showing what changed and which queries flipped to cited.
